The problem

Every capital event means the same manual work.

A notice arrives. Someone opens Excel, does the math, writes the memos, and sets up the wires by hand. Then it happens again next quarter.

  1. 01

    Reading PDFs and re-entering data

    Notices arrive as PDFs. Someone reads them, pulls out the numbers, and types them into a spreadsheet.
  2. 02

    Calculating allocations in Excel

    Ownership percentages, pro-rata shares, and rounding — all done by hand. One wrong cell affects every LP.
  3. 03

    Writing notices one at a time

    Capital call memos and distribution notices drafted from templates in Word. Copy, paste, update — once per LP, every time.
  4. 04

    Setting up wires manually

    Bank details pulled from a spreadsheet and entered by hand. No check against prior instructions, no protection if the details have changed.
